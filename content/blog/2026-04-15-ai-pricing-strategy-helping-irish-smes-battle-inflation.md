## Why Traditional Pricing Fails in 2024

Most Irish SMEs still use pricing strategies that were developed for a very different economic era. Here's why they're failing:

**Static pricing ignores market reality**: You set your prices once, maybe adjust them annually, and hope for the best. But market conditions change daily. During off-season, you're leaving money on the table. During peak periods, you might be undercharging compared to what customers would actually pay.

**Cost-plus pricing is a trap**: You calculate your costs, add a margin, and that's your price. This approach doesn't account for demand fluctuations, competitive dynamics, or customer willingness to pay. When costs rise, you have to choose between cutting your margin or raising prices — both painful options.

**Competitor watching is reactive**: You look at what competitors charge and adjust accordingly. But this is a race to the bottom. And it's based on assumptions about what competitors are doing that may or may not be accurate.

**Manual pricing is time-consuming**: Trying to optimises prices based on all these factors manually is simply impossible for a busy SME owner. You don't have the time or the data analysis skills to do it properly.

The result? Irish SMEs are either leaving significant revenue on the table or pricing themselves out of the market. Neither is acceptable when margins are already tight.

## How AI Transforms Your Pricing Strategy

AI-powered pricing isn't about simply raising prices. It's about making smarter pricing decisions that protect your margins while maintaining — or even growing — your customer base.

Here's how it works:

**Dynamic pricing based on real demand**: AI systems analyse your sales data, booking patterns, seasonal trends, and competitor information to recommend optimal prices for each situation. A hotel can charge more during festival weekends in Killarney and less during quiet midweek periods. A restaurant can adjust lunch menu pricing based on weather, day of week, and historical demand.

**Cost tracking and margin protection**: AI tools can automatically factor in cost increases and flag when margins fall below your target threshold. Rather than manually calculating whether a price increase is needed, you get alerts when conditions change.

**Customer segmentation and willingness to pay**: AI can identify which customers are most price-sensitive and which are willing to pay more for premium service. This lets you target discounts strategically rather than offering them to everyone.

**Competitor intelligence**: AI can monitor competitor pricing in real time and help you respond strategically. If a competitor drops prices, you know about it immediately and can decide how to respond.

**Demand forecasting**: AI analyses historical data to predict future demand with remarkable accuracy. This lets you price proactively rather than reactively.

The key insight is that AI enables what we call "smart pricing" — prices that reflect the actual value and demand at any given moment, rather than a static number that may or may not be appropriate.

## Practical Steps to Implement AI Pricing

Here's how your business can get started with AI-powered pricing:

**Step 1: Get your data in order**

You need to understand your current pricing performance before you can improve it. Look at your sales data — what are you selling, when, at what price, and to whom? Most Irish SMEs already have this data in their point-of-sale systems, but it's often not analysed properly. Spend some time understanding what your data tells you about pricing patterns.

**Step 2: Identify your pricing variables**

What factors affect what you can charge? For a hotel, it's the date, day of week, seasonality, events, and competitor pricing. For a restaurant, it's meal period, day of week, weather, and special occasions. For retail, it's product category, seasonality, and stock levels. Write these down.

**Step 3: Choose your starting point**

You don't need to implement full AI pricing overnight. Start with one area where pricing improvements would have the biggest impact. For most businesses, this is either their most profitable product or service, or their biggest revenue driver. Get that working first, then expand.

**Step 4: Select your tools**

There are AI pricing tools available for businesses of every size. Some are comprehensive platforms designed for larger operations. Others are simpler tools that integrate with your existing systems. At AIMediaFlow, we help Irish SMEs identify the right tools for their specific needs and budgets — starting as low as €50 per month for basic dynamic pricing tools.

**Step 5: Set clear rules**

AI pricing works best when it's guided by clear business rules. What's your minimum margin? What's your maximum price increase? Under what circumstances should you discount? Define these parameters so the AI operates within boundaries you're comfortable with.

**Step 6: Monitor and adjust**

AI pricing isn't set-and-forget. Review the results regularly. Is the system recommending prices that make sense? Are you seeing the expected improvements? Use the first few months as a learning period to refine your approach.

**Step 7: Expand gradually**

Once you've seen results from your first area, expand to other products, services, or time periods. Most businesses find that AI pricing improvements compound over time as they optimise more of their business.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Will AI pricing make my prices look erratic?**

No — the system operates within parameters you set. You can limit how much prices can vary from day to day. For most businesses, the changes are subtle but significant. You're not jumping from €50 to €200 overnight; you're making incremental adjustments that add up over time.

**What if AI recommends a price that's higher than my competitors?**

The system will flag this, but you remain in control. If you believe a lower price is necessary to remain competitive, you can override the recommendation. The AI provides information and recommendations; you make the final decision.

**Is this only for large businesses?**

Not at all. Many AI pricing tools are designed for small businesses and cost far less than a full-time pricing manager. Entry-level tools start at around €50 per month, making this accessible for businesses of any size.

**What if I get it wrong?**

AI pricing systems learn from their mistakes. The more data they have, the better their recommendations become. And because the changes are typically incremental, a wrong decision has limited impact. You can always adjust your parameters if something isn't working.

**Will customers notice and be upset?**

In our experience, customers rarely notice gradual pricing adjustments. What they do notice is improved availability, better service, and more consistent quality — all things that become possible when you're not constantly squeezed on margin.
